Why Hero Swap exists

The "new hero every month" treadmill is over. The new system gives players a stable target: 18 specific heroes across 3 army types — 6 combo marches in total — to work toward. Hero Swap is how you redirect everything you've poured into your existing heroes into one of those 18 targets, same army type, one-time conversion.

The rules — read before you swap

Hero Swap is irreversible. These rules are enforced by the game client.

Eligibility

  • Same army type only — Army → Army, Air → Air, Navy → Navy. No cross-pool.
  • One swap per SOURCE hero — Po → Nova consumes Po.
  • One swap per TARGET hero — once anyone in your account swaps to Nova, no further swaps land on Nova. Quily must aim at a different Army target that hasn't been swapped to yet.
  • Heroes must be in Base (not on a march) to swap.
  • Irreversible. Plan it.

What converts to the target

  • Exclusive Skill chips — both equipped on the source AND sitting in your bag convert to the target's chip pool.
  • Universal Hero Shards spent on the source convert to the target's pool.
  • Hero XP spent on the source converts to the target's pool.
  • Net: every leveling resource you ever spent on the source is recovered as the target's currency.
  • Donor resets to 1★ Lv. 1 with 10 base Shards kept.

⚠ Hero Perks — the trap

Hero Perks transfer from the source onto the target, overwriting whatever perks the target currently has. If both heroes have full perks and you want to keep the target's perks, move the target's perks to a different hero BEFORE the swap. Otherwise they're gone.

The 3 pools · 18 targets · 6 combo trios

Each army type has 6 awakening targets, grouped into 2 combo trios of 3. When all 3 members of a trio deploy in the same march, the trio buff activates.

How awakening works

A whole new progression layer. Awakening adds up to +240,000 Fight Power per hero (+80k from Awakening Level, +120k from 4 maxed skills, +40k from Honor Wall). Currently only Phase I heroes are eligible.

Eligibility

  • 5★
  • Lv. 120
  • Exclusive Skill Lv. 5 equipped
  • One of the 6 Phase I heroes (Sara, Nova, Shen Suyu, Carna, Jett, Lophy)

Awakening Card economy

  • The Awakening Time event (regular events) delivers 1 Awakening Card chest per week.
  • 1 card = 1 hero awakening.
  • This is the F2P bottleneck on how many heroes you can awaken.

Per-skill Tier ladder

Each skill has tiers 1 → 10. Tier 1 unlocks automatically. Awakening Stones power the upgrades.

  • Tier 6 — unlocks an additional effect/buff on top of the base skill. The natural target.
  • Exclusive Skill Lv. 7 — boosts the applied buff variant on every awakening skill (the "ES7 swap").
  • Tier 10 — final tier, unlocks the Honor Wall extension.

Stones to advance each tier (per skill): 75 · 75 · 150 · 150 · 225 · 225 · 300 · 400 · 650 → 2,250 to Tier 10, 675 to Tier 6.

The big questions — recommended priorities

Awakening resources are scarce. These are the trade-offs every player has to think about. Recommendations below are the current best-guess for most players on Server 2864.

TL;DR — the play

Focus one march. Push Awakening Lv. 30. Then take each skill to Tier 6 in order.

  • Pick one trio in your strongest army type.
  • Push each trio member to Awakening Lv. 30 (unlocks all 4 skills).
  • Take their Skill 1 to Tier 6, then Skill 2 to Tier 6, then 3, then 4. Don't spread tiers thinly.
  • Hold off on ES7 and Lv. 30→40 climb until Tier 6 is locked in across all 4 skills.

Q1 · When should I pause pushing Awakening Level?

Awakening Lv. 30 is the sweet spot for most players — it unlocks all 4 Awakening Skills at Tier 1, which is where the Awakening Stone economy actually opens up.

The Lv. 30 → 40 climb costs 550 Hero Shards + 440 Lv. 1 Hero Skill chips for just +20,000 FP from levels. Those chips are the same currency used to push the existing Exclusive Skill — they're better spent elsewhere until the rest of your awakening progression is locked in.

Pause at Lv. 30 for most heroes. Return to push Lv. 30 → 40 only after Tier 6 is reached on all 4 skills.

Q2 · Should I aim for Exclusive Skill 7, or stop at ES5 and focus on awakening?

ES5 (81 chips) is the awakening prerequisite — non-negotiable. ES7 (729 chips) is 9× the chip cost and unlocks the upgraded applied-buff variant on every Awakening Skill (meaningful damage boost) plus boosts the existing ES directly.

The 648-chip difference between ES5 and ES7 is the same currency that fuels Awakening Lv. 10–40. If you stop at ES5, you only need 81 + 740 = 821 chips per hero for full awakening. If you push ES7 AND Lv. 40, you need 1,469 chips per hero.

ES5 first, defer ES7 until Tier 6 is locked on all 4 awakening skills. ES7's payoff is the buff variant, but you need the skills there to benefit.

Q3 · Awakened march vs many smaller backup marches?

A fully awakened hero is +240,000 FP. A full march of 5 awakened heroes is up to +1.2M FP. The trio synergy bonus ONLY activates when all 3 specific combo-trio heroes deploy in the same march — spread heroes, lose the bonus.

Spreading awakening resources across multiple marches dilutes the per-hero FP gain AND breaks the trio synergy. Backup marches don't need the awakening layer to keep being useful.

One focused awakened march wins. Backup marches stay strong without awakening.

Q4 · What should my target order be?

Within a single hero:

  1. Awaken (5★, Lv. 120, ES5, 1 Awakening Card)
  2. Push Awakening Lv. → 30 (unlock all 4 skills)
  3. Skill 1 → Tier 6 (675 stones)
  4. Skill 2 → Tier 6 (675 stones)
  5. Skill 3 → Tier 6 (675 stones)
  6. Skill 4 → Tier 6 (675 stones) — 2,700 stones total, all bonus effects active
  7. Then consider ES7, Tier 10s, Awakening Lv. 40, Honor Wall
Don't spread tiers thinly. Tier 6 unlocks the bonus effect, so finish one skill to Tier 6 before opening the next.
