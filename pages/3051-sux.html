How the Power Index works ▼

Every candidate gets a single Power Index score (0–100) so two servers can be compared at a glance. The blend rewards depth and SSC performance while giving a modest discount to cheaper tickets, so the cost-100 column isn’t just “free filler”.

score = ( 0.50 · meritNorm + 0.25 · wzNorm + 0.15 · depthNorm ) × 100 × costMul
  • meritNorm — that server’s total Total Merit sum, divided by the highest in the candidate pool. Weighted heaviest because merit is the single best proxy for what an ally can put on the battlefield.
  • wzNorm — their UD/Seal Stone warzone score, normalised the same way. Captures how well the whole sector has been performing this season.
  • depthNorm — how many of their players show up in the cross-server merit rankings (top-12k). Wide rosters mean the ally still functions when a star or two no-shows.
  • costMul — 1.00 at 300 tickets, 1.05 at 200, 1.15 at 100. A small nudge so the cheap tier doesn’t look worse than it really is.

The Recommended next picks in the simulator use the same numbers in a budget-bounded search, picking the best combination of remaining candidates that still fits the budget and the 4-pick cap.

This is a quick weighted average, not the literal in-game battle outcome model — the actual fight is server-vs-server cell capture, so morale and timezone overlap matter too. Use the index as a starting point, not a verdict.
