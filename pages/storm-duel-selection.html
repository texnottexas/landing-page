How the Power Index works ▼

Every candidate gets a single Power Index score (0–100) so two warzones can be compared at a glance. The blend rewards player strength (merit) and the combat buffs a warzone brings from the wastelands it holds in its home sector, plus a light read on current Kartz Trial form, while giving a small discount to cheaper tickets so affordable allies aren’t penalised.

score = ( 0.296 · meritNorm + 0.22 · top5Norm + 0.13 · wzNorm + 0.064 · depthNorm + 0.25 · combatNorm + 0.04 · kartzNorm ) × 100 × costMul
  • meritNorm - that warzone’s total Seal Stone merit across its ranked players, divided by the highest in the candidate pool. Heaviest weight because merit is the best proxy for what an ally can put on the battlefield.
  • top5Norm - mean merit of their top 5 ranked players, normalised the same way. Catches warzones whose top end punches above their headline number.
  • combatNorm - how many combat wastelands (All Units ATK / HP / DMG+ / DMG− / DEF) the warzone holds in its home sector, divided by the pool max. Each warzone fights as its home sector and brings those passive buffs into the duel, so this is a real second factor. It is an honest count - the game does not expose wasteland upgrade level in bulk, so counts are not tier-weighted.
  • wzNorm - their Storm Duel / Seal Stone warzone score. Captures how the whole sector has been performing this season.
  • depthNorm - how many of their players show up in the cross-server merit rankings. Wide rosters mean the ally still functions when a star or two no-shows.
  • kartzNorm - a light read on current Kartz Trial form: 0.6 × their strongest alliance’s score + 0.4 × their best player’s standing in the global top 500, normalised to the pool. Carries the smallest weight (0.04) on purpose — just enough to break near-ties toward warzones showing strong live form, added for R15.
  • costMul - 1 + (300 − ticket) / 300 × 0.15, so 1.00 at 300 tickets, 1.05 at 200, 1.10 at 100. A small nudge so the cheap tier doesn’t look worse than it really is.

The simulator surfaces two recommendations on every turn:

  • Primary - the highest-Power-Index combination where every pick costs at least 200 tickets. This biases against grabbing 100-cost allies early, since they are likely to still be on the board when our next turn comes around.
  • Alternate - a single next-best candidate (by Power Index) that isn’t already in Primary and isn’t taken. The “why” line names the Primary pick you’d swap it for so it’s clear what trade you’re making.

This is a quick weighted average, not the literal in-game battle outcome model - the actual fight is warzone-vs-warzone cell capture, so morale and timezone overlap matter too. Use the index as a starting point, not a verdict.

How the draft works ▼
  • Each side has a 1000-ticket budget and can pick up to 4 allies from 16 candidates.
  • Tickets cost 300 / 200 / 100 by candidate tier (a couple sit just off the round numbers - cards always show the real cost).
  • We won the dice roll, so we pick on rounds 1 3 5; the opponent picks on rounds 2 4.
  • Round sizes are 1 → 2 → 2 → 2 → 1. Both sides end with 4 picks total.
  • Ally selection opens Jun 25, 16:00 UTC; the fight runs Jun 27, 14:00–15:00 UTC.
